Lower East Side, New York City

Tourists flock to this hip, downtown Manhattan neighborhood.

The eclectic Lower East Side is where gritty alleys and tenement-style buildings mix with upscale apartments and chic boutiques. Nighttime draws hip, young crowds to the area's trendy bars, music venues and restaurants. The neighborhood's Jewish heritage lives on through Orchard Street's Lower East Side Tenement Museum and old-world fabric stores, as well as traditional delis such as Katz's and Russ and Daughters. Russ and Daughters

Russ and Daughters is a treasure with both a rich selection of quality take away food and great service. Recommended are the salmon pastrami, coffee, babka ice cream sandwich and the "Fancy Delancey" sandwich which features smoked tuna, horseradish dill cream cheese and wasabi flying fish roe. Anthony Bourdain had it right when he said "Russ and Daughters" occupies that rare and tiny place on the mountaintop reserved for those who are not just the oldest and the last - but also the best." Russ and Daughters is located at 127 Orchard Street, between Rivington and Delancey Streets.

Tiny's Giant Sandwich Shop

Unpretentious and friendly, Tiny's is named for its original owner who was a man large in stature and made sure there were delicious options for both meat eaters and vegetarians alike. It's a great place to grab lunch, a coffee or a casual dinner. You can order ahead and roll up to the side window on Norfolk and pick up your order in a flash. Great when you're hungry and need to walk the dog. Try the Big Mac Daddy, Tiny's Tasty Happy Salad or the Spicy Rizzak Sandwich. 129 Rivington Street.
